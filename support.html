How it works

A resolution is what we call a decision in progress. You add your options, invite your group, and vote until a clear answer emerges. When it's done, you've settled something together.
Two options appear at a time. You pick one. This repeats until only one option remains. Votes stay hidden until everyone in the group has weighed in, then the result simply arrives.
No. Your votes are always private. The group sees the outcome, never who voted for what.
The person who started the resolution casts the deciding vote. It stays quiet and the result moves forward.
Ask a Friend is a one-to-one mode where you share your options with someone you trust and let them work through them on your behalf. They don't decide for you. They just help you see things a little more clearly. Their choices along the way stay completely private.
Pick for Me is a solo feature for when you're drawing a complete blank. Set your filters once and the app suggests something warm and considered. You can ask for a different suggestion up to three times. No pressure to take any of them.
