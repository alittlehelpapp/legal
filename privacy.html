This Privacy Policy explains how Little Frisser LLC (“we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, and protects your information when you use A Little Help (the “app”). We’ve written it in plain language because we believe you deserve to understand exactly what’s happening with your data — not just acknowledge a wall of legal text.

If something here isn’t clear, email us at support@a-little-help.com and we’ll explain it.

1. Who we are

A Little Help is operated by Little Frisser LLC, a company registered in the State of Oregon, United States. We built this app to make group decision-making less stressful — not to harvest your data.

2. What we collect and why

2.1 Account information

When you create an account, we collect your display name, username, email address, and password. Passwords are hashed via Supabase Auth — we never store them in readable form. We also collect optional fields: age group, gender identity, and city.

In plain terms: we need your email to identify your account and let you log back in. The rest is optional and helps us understand our users at an aggregate level.

2.2 Location data

Location is accessed only when the app is open and only if you’ve granted permission. We use it to find nearby restaurants, events, and activities. We don’t store your precise location history.

In plain terms: we look up what’s near you when you’re using the app. We don’t track where you go.

2.3 Contact matching

At launch, friends are discovered by searching their @username directly in the app. Contact-based friend matching is not available at launch and will be introduced in a future update, at which point this policy will be updated to reflect how that data is handled.

In plain terms: your contacts are not accessed at launch. When contact matching is added in a future update, we will update this section before the feature ships.

2.4 Resolution and decision data

We store the items you nominate, your resolution history, and resolution outcomes. Votes are stored as aggregate totals only — for example, 2–1. We never record which individual voted for which option. During the adding picks phase, other group members can see that you’ve added picks, but not what those picks are. When adding a custom pick, you may optionally attach a URL link for reference. If provided, that link is stored with the pick and is visible to all members of the resolution.

In plain terms: your specific votes are private. The group sees outcomes, not individual decisions.

2.5 Ask a Friend — a specific privacy promise

In Ask a Friend mode, the helper’s individual decision path through the resolution is completely private. The creator only ever sees the final result and the overall standings. We never share the helper’s step-by-step choices with the creator or anyone else. This is a core privacy commitment of the feature.

In plain terms: if someone asks you to help them decide, the creator will never know how you worked through the options — only where you landed.

2.6 Saved items and preferences

We store your saved favourites, custom picks, items marked “not for me,” notification preferences, theme settings, accessibility preferences, analytics consent state, and supporter status. These are stored server-side so they persist across devices and sessions.

2.7 Push notification tokens

We store your push notification token so we can send you alerts — such as when it’s your turn to vote, or when a friend invites you to a resolution. Tokens are updated on every app open. If a token is invalidated, it is cleared from our database immediately.

2.8 Post-decision check-in

After completing a resolution, you may be shown a brief check-in question asking how you feel about the outcome. This applies across all three modes — Ask a Friend, group consensus, and Pick for Me — with questions and timing appropriate to each. In Ask a Friend, the check-in includes a privacy note confirming the helper will never see your response. In group mode, the check-in is shown to the creator only. In Pick for Me, the check-in appears on your next app open after using the feature.

Responses are stored as anonymous aggregates only — no user ID, resolution ID, or helper ID is ever associated with a response. If you select ‘Ask me later,’ the check-in is deferred and resurfaces at the start of your next session in the same mode. If you have not returned within 2–3 weeks, the deferred response is discarded automatically.

In plain terms: check-in responses are private to you and us. In Ask a Friend, your helper never sees them. In group mode, other members never see them. Responses are always anonymous aggregates.

2.9 Analytics

We generate anonymous aggregate analytics nightly from completed resolution data. We never write user IDs to analytics tables. A minimum threshold of 10 resolutions per demographic segment is enforced before any analytics row is created — this prevents individual data points from being re-identified.

Analytics collection requires your explicit opt-in during onboarding. The default state is opted out. You can change this at any time in Privacy & Data settings.

2.10 Supporter tier

If you choose to support the app financially, payment is handled entirely by RevenueCat. We never process or store payment card data. We store your supporter status and a personal heart icon visible only to you in your own profile — never to other users.

3. How we use your information

  • To operate the app and provide its features
  • To suggest nearby content when location permission is granted
  • To send notifications you’ve chosen to receive
  • To find friends by @username search
  • To improve the app through anonymous aggregate analytics (only if you’ve opted in)
  • To process voluntary supporter contributions via RevenueCat
  • To respond to your support requests and content reports

We do not use your data to serve advertising. We do not sell your data to third parties. Ever.

4. Third-party services

We work with the following services to deliver the app. Each has its own privacy policy.

  • Supabase — database and authentication, US-East region
  • Expo — push notifications via Apple Push Notification Service (APNs) and Firebase Cloud Messaging (FCM)
  • RevenueCat — in-app purchases and subscription management
  • TMDB — movie and TV data. This product uses the TMDB API but is not endorsed or certified by TMDB.
  • Google Places API — restaurant data. Powered by Google, displayed in-app per their requirements.

We share only the minimum data necessary for each service to function.

5. Voting and nomination privacy

5.1 Vote privacy

Votes are never individually attributed. All members see the aggregate result (e.g., 2–1) — never who voted for what. This applies to all resolution modes.

5.2 Nomination privacy during pick collection

While a resolution is in the pick-collection phase, other members can see that you’ve added picks (name, status, and count), but not what you’ve nominated. Options are revealed only when voting begins.

5.3 Ask a Friend privacy

As stated in section 2.5, the helper’s individual decision path is completely private. The creator sees only the final result and standings — never the helper’s step-by-step choices.

6. Invites and searchability

Any user can be found by searching their @username. Being findable does not add anyone to anything — all invites and friend requests require your explicit acceptance. Resolution invites expire after 72 hours if not accepted or declined.

7. Minimum age

A Little Help is an 18+ application and is not intended for use by anyone under 18 years of age. Users must confirm they are at least 18 years old via a required checkbox before creating an account. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 18. If you believe a person under 18 has created an account, please contact us at support@a-little-help.com and we will delete the account promptly.

8. Your rights and controls

8.1 Edit your data

You can update your display name, username, email, city, age group, and gender at any time in the app.

8.2 Delete your data

You can delete your account at any time from Security settings. All personal data is permanently purged after 30 days via an automated job. At day 25, we’ll send a reactivation notice to your registered email. Anonymous analytics contributions (which contain no PII) are retained after deletion.

8.3 Saved items, resolution history, and custom picks

You can delete individual saved items, custom picks, and resolution history within the app at any time.

8.4 Analytics opt-out

You can opt out of analytics at any time via the Privacy & Data toggle in settings. Users who opt out are excluded from all analytics aggregation.

8.5 Notifications

You can independently toggle each notification type — resolution invites, voting alerts, pick reminders, tiebreak alerts, friend requests, resolution updates, helper requests, and support prompts — in Notification settings at any time.

8.6 Permissions

Location and notification permissions can be revoked at any time via your device’s system settings.

8.7 Data export

You can request a copy of your data by emailing support@a-little-help.com with the subject line “Data request.” We’ll fulfil your request manually within a reasonable time.

8.8 Data access

You can request details of all data we hold about you by emailing support@a-little-help.com with the subject line “Data access request.”

9. Data retention

We retain your data while your account is active. If you delete your account, personal data is permanently purged 30 days after deletion. Anonymous analytics contributions are retained indefinitely. Push notification tokens that become invalid are cleared immediately.

10. Security

We use industry-standard practices to protect your data, including password hashing via Supabase Auth, encrypted data transmission, and access controls. No method of data transmission or storage is 100% secure — but we take this seriously and will notify you promptly if we become aware of a breach affecting your data.

11. Grandfathering commitment

All users who create an account before any future change to a paid model will retain free access to all features available at the time of their sign-up, permanently. This is a technical guarantee stored in our database — not just a promise on a webpage. Your account_created_at timestamp and grandfathered status are recorded at sign-up and are never altered.

12. Oregon Consumer Privacy Act

The Oregon Consumer Privacy Act (OCPA), effective July 2025, applies to companies processing the personal data of 100,000 or more Oregon consumers, or 25,000 or more consumers where data sales generate revenue. A Little Help will be well under these thresholds at launch. We are committed to reassessing this as our user base grows and will update this policy and our practices if we approach these thresholds.

13. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes to this Privacy Policy, we’ll notify you via push notification and an in-app banner at least 30 days before the changes take effect. Continued use of the app after that date constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy. Non-material changes won’t require notice, but the effective date at the top of this document will always reflect the most recent revision.

14. Contact

Questions, concerns, or requests: support@a-little-help.com

Note for EU users

A Little Help is not currently available in the European Union. The app is restricted to the United States in both the Apple App Store and Google Play during this initial period. We plan to expand to the EU as a post-launch workstream. Before we do, we are committed to addressing the following requirements under the GDPR and applicable national laws.

Lawful basis

We will document a lawful basis for each category of processing under GDPR Article 6 — likely including consent (analytics, notifications), contract performance (core functionality), and legitimate interests (security).

Data processor agreements

We will put in place Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) with all sub-processors (Supabase, Expo, RevenueCat, and the content API providers listed in section 4) before processing EU personal data.

Right to portability

Before EU expansion, we will implement a self-serve data export tool rather than the manual email process currently in place.

Age gate requirements

A Little Help requires users to be at least 18 years of age, which exceeds the minimum age of consent for data processing in all EU member states (which ranges from 13 to 16 depending on the country). Before EU expansion, we will confirm that our age verification implementation satisfies any additional local requirements.

Data residency

Supabase currently operates in US-East. Before EU expansion, we will evaluate whether EU-region hosting is required and configure it accordingly.
