A Little Help

Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 2026

This Privacy Policy explains what data the A Little Help app collects, why we collect it, how we use it, and what rights you have over it. It applies to the A Little Help app (the "App"), operated by Little Frisser LLC ("we", "us", or "our"), a limited liability company registered in the State of Oregon.

We've tried to write this in plain language. Where we've had to use legal phrasing, you'll find a plain-language summary directly below it in italics.

1. Who we are

Little Frisser LLC operates the A Little Help app. If you have any questions about this policy, you can reach us at alittlehelpapp@proton.me.

We're a small team. There's a real person at that email address.

2. Who this policy applies to

This policy applies to anyone who creates an account or uses the App. The App is available in the United States only. You must be at least 13 years old to use the App. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13.

If you're under 13, please don't use the app. If you're a parent and believe your child has created an account, contact us and we'll delete it right away.

3. What data we collect — and why

3.1 Account data

When you create an account, we collect:

  • Display name
  • Username
  • Email address
  • Password — stored as a hashed credential via Supabase Auth. We never see your actual password.
  • Optional: age group, gender, and city — used to improve suggestions and for anonymous aggregate analytics. These are never shared with third parties.

You must confirm you are 13 years of age or older via a required checkbox before account creation. This applies to all sign-up methods including Google and Apple SSO.

3.2 Location data

If you grant permission, we use your device's location while the App is open to surface nearby restaurants, events, and activities. We do not store your location on our servers. Location access is always optional — the App works without it, with some features limited.

We use location to show you relevant content nearby. We don't track where you go or log your location history.

3.3 Contacts

If you grant permission, we compare your phone contacts against registered usernames to suggest people you might know on the App. Your contacts are never uploaded to or stored on our servers. This comparison happens on-device only.

We'll help you find friends — but your contact list stays on your phone.

3.4 Push notification token

We store your device's push notification token and update it each time you open the App. If your token is invalidated (for example, after a device reset), it is cleared from our database immediately.

3.5 Preferences and settings

We store your app preferences server-side, including: theme (light/dark/system), accessibility preferences, notification preferences for each notification type, analytics consent state, and support prompt state.

3.6 Bracket and decision data

We store all bracket data associated with your account, including:

  • Items you nominate to brackets
  • Votes you cast — recorded as aggregate totals only (e.g. 2–1). Votes are never individually attributed to you. No one, including us, can see how you voted.
  • Bracket history, including completed and paused brackets

Your vote is yours. We show group results as numbers — never as 'Jamie voted for this.'

3.7 Saved content and custom picks

We store items you've saved as favourites, custom picks you've added, liked and disliked items, and custom categories. You can delete any of these at any time within the App.

3.8 Analytics

We generate anonymous aggregate analytics nightly from completed bracket data. These analytics never include your user ID or any personally identifiable information. A minimum group threshold of 10 brackets per demographic segment is enforced before any analytics row is written — this prevents re-identification.

Analytics participation is opt-in. The default state is opted out. You will be asked during onboarding whether you'd like to contribute. You can change this at any time in Privacy & Data settings.

We only look at patterns across groups — never at individual users. And we only look if you've told us we can.

3.9 Payment data

The App offers an optional voluntary supporter tier. Payment processing is handled entirely by RevenueCat, which wraps Apple StoreKit and Google Play Billing. We never handle, see, or store payment card data.

4. How we share your data

We do not sell your data. We do not share your data with third parties for advertising purposes. Full stop.

We use the following third-party services to operate the App. Each receives only the data they need to perform their function:

  • Supabase — database and authentication (US-East region)
  • Expo — push notifications via APNs (Apple) and FCM (Google)
  • RevenueCat — in-app purchases
  • TMDB — movie and TV content data
  • Google Places API — restaurant data. "Powered by Google" attribution is displayed in the App per Google's requirements.
  • Google Books API — book data
  • Ticketmaster and Eventbrite — events data
  • Railway — backend server and scheduled jobs
  • Cloudflare — CDN and image caching

"This product uses the TMDB API but is not endorsed or certified by TMDB." This attribution is displayed on the App's About screen.

These are infrastructure providers, not advertising or data broker partners. They process your data only to make the App work.

5. Privacy within the App

5.1 Voting privacy

Votes in brackets are shown as aggregate totals only — for example, 2–1. No one can see how you individually voted, and we never store votes in a way that is linked to your identity.

5.2 Nomination privacy

During the adding picks phase, group members can see that others have added picks, and how many — but not what those picks are. Pick details are hidden until voting begins.

5.3 A Little Help (Ask a friend) mode

In A Little Help mode, one user nominates all items and asks a trusted friend to work through the options and make decisions on their behalf. The helper sees all nominated items and knows who nominated them (it's always the one person who invited them). The helper must explicitly accept before participating. This is a consensual peer-to-peer interaction — we facilitate it, but both parties choose to take part.

5.4 Invites and bracket access

You can be invited to a bracket by any App user. You must explicitly accept or decline before being added. Accepting a bracket invite is not the same as a friend request. Unaccepted invites expire automatically after 72 hours.

5.5 Username searchability

Any App user can find you by searching your @username. Being findable does not add you to anything — all bracket invites and friend requests require your explicit acceptance.

5.6 Supporter status

The supporter heart icon is visible only to you — it is never displayed to other users.

6. Data retention

We retain your data for as long as your account is active. If you delete your account, a deletion timestamp is set immediately and all personal data is permanently and irreversibly purged after 30 days via an automated scheduled job.

Anonymous analytics contributions are retained after deletion. These contain no PII and cannot be traced back to you.

Paused brackets are retained indefinitely until the bracket creator reactivates or deletes them.

If a bracket creator deletes their account: for group consensus and turn-based brackets, creator status transfers to the next longest-standing member. A Little Help brackets are cancelled and the helper is notified. The deleted user's nominated items remain in active brackets as ownerless nominations.

7. Your rights and controls

Edit your profile

You can update your display name, username, email, and city at any time. Age group and gender are optional and can also be updated at any time.

Delete your data

You can delete saved items, bracket history, and custom picks within the App. You can delete your account at any time via Settings → Security → Delete account.

Opt out of analytics

You can change your analytics consent at any time in Privacy & Data settings. Opting out takes effect immediately — you will be excluded from all future analytics aggregation.

Revoke permissions

You can revoke location, contacts, and notification permissions at any time via your device's system settings.

Request a copy of your data

Privacy & Data settings includes a 'Request a copy of your data' option. Tapping it opens a pre-populated email to alittlehelpapp@proton.me. We'll fulfil requests manually at launch.

Request access to your data

Privacy & Data settings also includes a 'Request access to your data' option, fulfilled the same way.

Notification controls

You can independently control notifications for each type: bracket invites, voting alerts, pick reminders, tiebreak alerts, friend requests, bracket updates, helper requests, group updates, and support prompts.

8. Oregon Consumer Privacy Act

Oregon's Consumer Privacy Act (effective July 2025) applies to companies processing data of 100,000 or more Oregon consumers, or 25,000 or more consumers where data sale is a revenue source. At launch, A Little Help will be well below both thresholds, and we do not sell data. We will reassess as our user base grows and implement the Act's requirements as they become applicable.

9. Children's privacy

The App is intended for users aged 13 and older. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13. A required age confirmation checkbox is present at sign-up. If you believe a child under 13 has created an account, please contact us at alittlehelpapp@proton.me and we will delete the account.

10. Security

Passwords are hashed and never stored in plain text. Data is transmitted over encrypted connections. Push tokens are cleared on invalidation. We take reasonable technical precautions to protect your data — but no system is perfectly secure, and we can't guarantee absolute security.

11. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes to this policy, we'll notify you via in-app banner and push notification before the changes take effect. Continued use of the App after the effective date constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

Note for EU users

Important: A Little Help is not currently available in the European Union. This section describes what we would need to address before any EU expansion and is provided for transparency.

If we expand to EU markets, the following requirements under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) would apply and would be addressed before launch:

Lawful basis documentation

We would need to document a lawful basis for each category of processing (e.g. contract performance, legitimate interests, or consent). Our current analytics opt-in model already aligns with consent-based processing.

Data processor agreements

We would need to enter into Data Processing Agreements (DPAs) with each third-party service listed in Section 4 of this policy.

Right to portability

GDPR Article 20 requires a self-serve data export mechanism. Our current manual fulfilment process would need to be replaced with an automated export feature before EU launch.

Age gate requirements by member state

GDPR Article 8 sets the minimum age for consent at 16, but allows member states to lower this to 13. Requirements vary by country. A compliant age gate and, in some jurisdictions, parental consent mechanism would be required.

Data residency

Depending on the member states we launch in, data residency requirements or data transfer mechanisms (such as Standard Contractual Clauses) may apply. Supabase's EU-West region would likely be required.

Data Protection Officer

Depending on the scale of processing, we may be required to appoint a Data Protection Officer.

This section does not constitute compliance with GDPR. The App is not currently available in the EU, and EU residents are not permitted to create accounts at this time.
